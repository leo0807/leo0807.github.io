Technical writing works best when it explains what matters first, then expands into the details.

For AI systems, that means describing the problem, the system boundaries, the tradeoffs, and the results in a way that helps another engineer or product teammate understand the work quickly.

## A simple structure

1. State the problem.
2. Explain the approach.
3. Show the tradeoffs.
4. Close with what changed because of the work.

That pattern keeps the writing clear and makes it easier to keep adding posts later.
